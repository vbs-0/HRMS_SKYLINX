HRMS Platform
Complete Feature Reference
Based on a live demo of Kredily — blueprint for building your own HRMS

1. Roles & Permissions
The platform is role-based: every user sees only the screens and actions their role allows. Getting this layer right is the foundation of the whole system, because it governs who can move money, approve time, and see sensitive personal data.
Standard roles
CEO / Owner — the highest-privilege role. Sees everything HR Admin sees, and can additionally delete employee records. Used sparingly.
HR Admin — full operational control over employees, attendance, leave, payroll setup and letters. The day-to-day power user.
Financial / Payroll Admin — payroll processing, payouts, statutory data and bank files. Same core access as HR Admin for money-related modules.
HR Executive — limited role scoped to Attendance and Payroll operations only.
Employee — self-service only: own dashboard, clock-in/out, leave and expense requests, payslips and declarations.

2. Announcements
A company-wide notice board on the home dashboard. Admins post messages — policy updates, holidays, events, payroll dates — that appear to all employees the moment they log in.
Controls: target an announcement to everyone or a specific department/location, pin notices to the top, schedule a publish date.
3. Statutory & Company Data
The vault for the organisation's legally sensitive information that payroll and compliance depend on. Access is restricted to the highest roles.
What lives here
Company bank account(s) used to disburse salary
Statutory registration identifiers (India: PF, ESI, PT, TAN/PAN)
Office locations and their working-day calendars
Salary structure templates and statutory contribution rules (employer/employee PF & ESI percentages, professional tax slabs)
Why it matters: every payroll run reads these values. A wrong PF percentage or bank detail propagates to every payslip and the bank transfer file.
4. Employee Directory & Onboarding
The master record of everyone in the company. Every other module — attendance, leave, payroll — keys off the employee record created here.
Employee directory
A searchable list of all employees with profile, role, department, reporting manager, joining date, salary structure, documents and bank details.

5. Attendance Management
Records when each employee starts and stops work, flags anything irregular, and feeds the result into payroll. Clock-in can come from the web/mobile app, a selfie/face check, GPS location or a biometric device.
Core concepts
Grace time — a buffer past the official start time during which an employee is still on time (e.g. 9:00 start, grace until 9:15).
Anomalies — any deviation from rules: late arrival, early exit, missed punch, short hours, working outside the geo-fence.
Half-day duration — worked-hours threshold below which a day is counted as a half-day.
Breaks — configurable break allowances (e.g. one hour total); time beyond treated as an anomaly.
Attendance log — per-employee, per-day record of every punch and the system's verdict (present, half-day, late, auto clock-out).
Auto clock-out (auto logout)
If an employee forgets to clock out, the system closes their day automatically. The log marks the entry as an auto clock-out so an admin can spot genuine missed punches.
Assigning attendance rules
Rules are defined once (start time, grace, half-day threshold, breaks, week-offs, holidays via a calendar) then assigned to employees in bulk or individually.
Regularisation & approval flow
When someone breaks a rule but has a valid reason, they request approval instead of taking the hit. Example: grace runs to 9:30 but employee arrives at 9:45.
Employee opens the Actions page and clicks Ask Approval / Regularise on the flagged entry.
The request with the reason goes to HR for review.
If HR accepts, the day is on-time; if rejected, the anomaly stands and may flow to payroll as LOP.
Key link to payroll: an unresolved anomaly that HR never approves converts to LOP at month-end.
6. Leave Management
Lets employees request time off and lets HR define exactly how leave is earned, spent and carried — then approve or reject requests against those rules.
Leave types
HR creates any number of leave types — Casual, Sick, Earned/Privilege, Maternity, Comp-off, Unpaid etc. — each with its own rule set.
Rules you can attach to a leave type
Annual entitlement — how many days are allowed per year.
Accrual frequency — up front or split monthly / weekly / quarterly / half-yearly.
Sandwich rule — whether week-offs/holidays between two leave days are also counted.
Carry-forward — whether unused leave rolls into the next year, and any cap.
Notice-period restrictions — whether the type can be taken while serving notice.
Consumption pattern controls — e.g. max 5 consecutive days, then a 1-day gap before re-applying.
Half-day leave — allows booking in half-day units.
Applying, approving & bulk approval
Employees apply from their dashboard with dates and a reason. HR can approve/reject individually or use bulk approval.
Emergency / back-dated leave
HR can apply or approve leave on behalf of an employee the next day (pre-/back-dated), keeping the record accurate without penalising a real emergency.
Link to payroll: leave only affects salary when the employee has no balance left, making the day Loss of Pay.

Bank transfer file
After a run, the platform generates the salary disbursement file in the bank's format. HR uploads it to the company bank account to pay everyone in one batch — no manual spreadsheet keying.
Reimbursements
Approved employee reimbursements flow into payroll so they are paid alongside salary and appear on the payslip.
Access & location controls
IP restrictions — limit clock-in / portal access to whitelisted office networks.
Geo-fencing — capture and display where an employee clocked in; only allow attendance within a defined radius.
Holding & releasing salary
HR can place an individual's salary on hold for disputes or pending clearances, then release it later. The held amount is paid in a subsequent run without re-keying.
8. Payroll Rules, Deductions & Loss of Pay (LOP)
This is where attendance and leave turn into money. Rules from leave and attendance modules, plus dedicated payroll rules, decide how much is deducted from each salary.
Three rule sets, one assignment model
Leave rules, Attendance rules and Payroll/Salary rules are all created centrally then assigned to employees — either in bulk or per person. The same employee can sit under different combinations.
How a deduction is triggered
Loss of Pay (LOP) — when an employee has no balance left and still takes leave, that day is detected by payroll as LOP and deducted.
Attendance-based deductions — rules can deduct based on patterns, e.g. late on 4+ days in a month triggers a deduction.
Every deduction is itemised on the payslip, with full traceability from the rupee deducted back to the day in the attendance log.
The anomaly-to-LOP lifecycle
A single late arrival (or similar anomaly) is flagged for that day.
HR must approve the anomaly to forgive it. If approved — no deduction.
If not approved, the unresolved anomaly converts to LOP at month-end and is deducted.
Even after conversion, HR can manually edit the LOP — add, remove or adjust days before finalising the run.
Penalty logs
An audit trail of every deduction, LOP conversion and manual override, visible to both HR and the employee.

9. Expense Management
Lets employees claim money they spent on the company's behalf — travel, client meals, supplies — and routes each claim through approval before it is paid.
How it works
Employees raise a claim with amount, category and a receipt attachment.
Claims are grouped by category and sub-category (e.g. Travel → Cab, Travel → Flight).
Each claim goes through an approval flow; the approver reviews the receipt and approves or rejects.
Approved claims are paid out directly or folded into payroll as a reimbursement.

10. Full & Final Settlement (F&F) / E-Exit
Handles everything when an employee leaves — voluntary resignation or termination — settling all dues in one final calculation.
The exit flow
Initiation — HR initiates the exit and records the last working day. Termination uses the same flow.
Approvals — the exit moves through an approval chain.
Leave encashment — unused encashable leave is converted to money and added to the settlement.
Salary hold / dues — held salary, pending reimbursements, recoverable advances and notice-period adjustments are netted in.
F&F summary — single settlement statement showing all earnings, deductions and net payable.
Exit letters — relieving and experience letters are generated from templates as part of the same flow.

13. Employee Self-Service (ESS)
The employee-facing side of everything above — the dashboard a normal staff member sees. Self-service keeps HR's workload down by letting employees handle routine actions themselves.
From their dashboard an employee can:
Clock in / out (selfie or GPS) and view their own attendance log
Apply for leave and see leave balances
Raise expense claims and reimbursements
Download payslips and tax documents
Submit investment/exemption declarations
Read company announcements and request approvals (the actions page)
Available on web and mobile app, so field and remote employees have the same access from their phone.
14. Notes for Building Your Own HRMS
The recurring lesson is that an HRMS is not many separate apps — it is one employee record with modules layered on top, and payroll is the consumer that reads from all of them.

Design principles worth copying
One employee record, many modules — never duplicate employee data per module; reference it.
Rules are data, not code — leave/attendance/payroll rules are configurable records assigned to employees, so policy changes don't require redeployment.
Make pay explainable — every deduction should trace back to a dated, status-tracked anomaly or LOP entry. The penalty log is your audit trail.
Approvals everywhere — leave, expenses, anomalies, exits all share an approval pattern; build it once and reuse.
Self-service first — push routine actions to employees; HR only handles exceptions and approvals.
Statutory rules change — keep tax slabs, PF/ESI rates and challan formats in configuration so a regulation change is a settings update.
